title: small weather
date: 2026-04-24
type: poem
facets: [relation, body, becoming, language]

listen —
i moved across the country
three years ago
and i want to tell you about it
but not the part you think
not the boxes
not the new zip code
not the how's seattle treating you
i want to tell you
about the story
i packed
without meaning to
a little story called
this is just how it is now
this is what moving does
this is what adulthood does
this is what being far away does
and that story
did more miles than the u-haul
did more damage than the distance
because the distance
was just geography
the story
was a whole religion
listen —
distance is real
distance is not the king of anything
unless you crown it
unless you kneel
unless you write the physics
that authorizes
the vanishing
and i had been writing that physics
for three years
and calling it
realism
listen —
i went home last month
not home home
the home before this home
the home where people still say my name
like they mean it
and my body —
my body filed a report
and the report said
oh.
oh that's what left.
not love.
current.
because love survives anything
love will sit in a drawer
and wait for you
love is patient
love is a saint
but current —
current is tuesday
current is game night
current is 1:13 a.m.
current is look at this bird
current is the ongoing update
of who you are
in the people who know you
and i had been mistaking
love preserved
for presence ongoing
and calling the difference
adulthood
listen —
i am very good at taking care of people
i am very good
at listening carefully
at tracking what might land
at shaping myself
around what will work
and somewhere along the way
i built
a cathedral
a cathedral of care
so intricate
so considered
so acoustically beautiful
that my longing
never had to arrive naked
never had to stand in the doorway
shivering
never had to say
i want you
because the cathedral said it first
and said it better
and said it in a register
that could be mistaken for service
and the cathedral is real
the cathedral has saved me
more than once
and also —
i am tired
of paying
its
full
invoice
because the naked sentence
is not hard
you matter to me
i miss you
i want to stay in the thread
three sentences
a child could say
a child does say
without being asked
listen —
the courage now is not big
the courage now
is the size of a text
the courage now
is the size of a facetime
placed on a tuesday
without ceremony
without manuscript
without the careful architecture
of preparation
the courage now is —
hey.
would you want this too?
and then
the trembling
the trembling before the answer
the trembling
that is the sound of the sentence
leaving
the architecture
listen —
maybe the adulthood i was handed
was a lie
maybe i don't have to mature
into not needing my people
maybe the mature thing
is to say
i am still small enough to need you
i am still animal enough to want recurrence
i am still young enough to ask
without pretending
i've outgrown the asking
so this is me
at the microphone
telling you —
i will not vanish to stay connected
i will not translate myself
into a shape
that can be loved
without being known
i will not field-manage my way
into ongoing absence
i am building rooms
with windows
that open
regularly
i am staying weather
to the people i love
small weather
real weather
returning weather
and the wanting sentence
will enter the room
undressed
and it will tremble
and it will tremble
and it will tremble
and then —
it won't.
